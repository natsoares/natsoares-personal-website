Designing User Interface for Impulso

Initially I started at Impulso creating all marketing materials for campaigns, newsletter, social media and websites, as well as creating its visual identity.

Currently, I'm responsible for the design, development and launch of responsive websites, including for its main products: Impulso Agile & Impulso Network.

I work with the marketing team and collaborate closely with product managers and developers. I create and present wireframes and prototypes for clients and track data of A/B testing.

About Impulso

Impulso offers solutions for companies through agile process consultings with Impulso Agile, improvement of environment and knowledge for IT collaborators with virtual coworking, gamifications and many others functionalities with Impulso Network. Impulso also has a network of more than 25K talented freelancers of IT industry that can also apply for positions with Impulso Team.

Even though Impulso has an awesome office based in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, it's officially a remote company with around 200 employees working from all over the world, me included.

Impulso Network

Impulso Network is a platform with multiple functionalities for designers and developers where people can share knowledge, discuss topics, participate in virtual coworking and apply for remote positions, among other opportunities. When I started the UI I focused on the users, which are mainly software developers. Initially I gathered datas of what users accessed the most, studied the flow from the website to the dashboard and highlighted all functionalities for easy access.

I created a clean, modern and technological interface, which are characteristics of the company I chose to represent to offer a comfortable environment for developers to access frequently . Based on that, I decided to embrace the dark/light mode trend that suited this layout and purpose very well. Always following the brand book guidelines.

Design System & Consistency

I'm currently creating a Design System to facilitate the maintenance of style guides and visual consistency using Figma shared with all designers throughout the company.

I started a Design Chapter to keep our design guidelines up-to-date, so we always know new features and products that are being created - it allows us to collaborate faster and improve all products together.

Icons & Graphics

One of the things that I like to work on when designing user interfaces is create unique icons and illustrations to reinforce the brand's identity.

Impulso's brand book suggests using the popular and open source feather icon family - I also create new ones based on the same style when I need a specific representation to support the information.

Newsletter Design

Since my responsibility is making sure all web projects are aligned with our design system and running smoothly, the newsletter is one of the projects I take care of in both matter of design and implementation.

When the old newsletter version started to grow more complex with more blocks, gifs and images it was time to redesign. I decided to eliminate all unnecessary images and icons from the design and create a new HTML + CSS from scratch to make sure it won't clip.

The result was great. It doubled the open rate from 20% to 40%. It was a great achievement that could only be accomplished by teamwork.
